Starting a quote

Files and estimates

What files can I send?

STL and 3MF files are preferred. If you have another format, email it and ask whether it can be reviewed.

Do I need K1C slicer numbers?

No. The calculator is the fastest way to get a ballpark estimate, but you can send the model directly for a manual quote.

Why can the final price differ from the calculator?

The calculator cannot see supports, orientation, geometry, print risk, or final machine settings. Those are checked before the price is confirmed.

What information should I include?

Attach the model and include your deadline, material or color preference, and approximate size if known.

Production

Materials and timing

What materials are available?

PLA is the normal default. PETG, TPU, and ABS may also be available depending on the part and current material stock.

How large can the part be?

The K1C build volume is 220 × 220 × 250 mm. Larger models may need to be split and quoted as multiple parts.

How long does printing take?

Standard jobs are usually ready 3–5 days after file approval and payment. Economy is usually 7–10 days. Priority and rush timing depend on capacity.

Can I request a specific color?

Yes. Special colors may affect price or timing if the material is not already available.

Completing the order

Payment, pickup, and reprints

When do I pay?

After you receive and approve the final price and completion estimate. Printing begins after payment.

How does pickup work?

You receive a message when the part is ready, then schedule pickup near the Student Union. Special meeting requests depend on availability.

What happens if a print fails?

Print-caused failures are evaluated for reprinting. Problems caused by the supplied model or incorrect dimensions may require a revised file and new quote.

Is the calculator price final?

No. It is a ballpark estimate. The final price is confirmed after the model is reviewed and before you pay.
