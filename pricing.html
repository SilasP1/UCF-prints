How estimates are calculated

Subtotal = $5 setup fee + ($0.10 per gram of filament) + ($1.50 per estimated print hour). Estimated total = subtotal multiplied by the selected lead-time multiplier, with a $12 minimum estimated order.

  • Priority, 1-2 days: 1.35x
  • Rush / same-day: 1.75x
  • Immediate / emergency: custom quote only

Why final quotes may change

This calculator is an estimate only. Final pricing may change after file review.

Geometry complexity

Intricate features can require slower printing, added review, and more careful setup.

Support material

Support-heavy models increase material use, cleanup effort, and print risk.

Failed-print risk

Unstable parts or difficult orientations may require added caution and scheduling buffers.

Tight tolerances

Precision-fit parts may need additional review, orientation planning, or iteration.

Large part size

Oversized parts can affect layout, print duration, and machine availability.

File repair needs

Non-manifold geometry or export issues can require prep work before printing.

Material/color availability

Specific stock requests may depend on current inventory and sourcing constraints.

Multiple parts

Batches and grouped jobs can change total material, setup time, and scheduling impact.

Fragile features or overhangs

Fragile features, thin walls, and steep overhangs often need extra support strategy.

Split-print or assembly needs

Large or complex builds may need multiple print stages and post-print assembly.

Deadline urgency

Tighter turnaround windows increase queue pressure and may require manual approval.

Pricing logic

Quotes are based on actual print effort

Every job is priced from the technical demands of the part instead of a flat one-size-fits-all rate.

Print time

Longer machine occupancy increases scheduling value and queue impact.

Material

Different filaments carry different stock costs and handling requirements.

Filament usage

Estimates account for the actual volume and waste profile of the job.

Complexity

Challenging geometry can require extra prep and more careful monitoring.

Supports

Support-heavy parts add setup, post-processing effort, and additional material use.

Deadline urgency

Tighter delivery windows shift scheduling priority and increase review intensity.

Long print policy

Special review for extended jobs

  • Maximum planned capacity is 12 print-hours per day.
  • Prints longer than 12 hours require manual review.
  • Extended jobs are marked as Special Quote Required.
