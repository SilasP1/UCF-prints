FAQ

Pricing questions, answered clearly

Key pricing logic and final-quote factors are here in a tighter format so the page stays easier to scan.

What drives the final quote?

Print time, material choice, filament usage, supports, geometry complexity, and deadline urgency all affect pricing after file review.

Why can final pricing change?

Geometry complexity, support material, failed-print risk, tight tolerances, large part size, file repair needs, material or color availability, and multi-part jobs can all change the final quote.

What counts as a special review?

Prints over 12 hours, unusual materials, fragile features, steep overhangs, split-print assembly needs, and rush scheduling often require manual review.

Why are rush jobs priced higher?

Rush work uses schedule flexibility, increases queue impact, and raises production risk compared with standard student jobs.

Why can UCF Prints still be affordable?

Standard prints are priced to stay student-friendly while local pickup, faster revisions, and no shipping cost help keep overall project cost down.

When is campus printing still the best option?

For small, non-urgent jobs, campus printing can be cheapest if a printer is available. UCF Prints becomes more valuable when deadlines, reliability, or queue risk matter.
