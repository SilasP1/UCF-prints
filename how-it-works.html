Our Process

UCF Prints is built to make 3D printing easier for students without turning every project into a printer setup lesson.

01

Submit Your Print Request

Start by sending your file or project details through the quote form. Include your material preference, color, deadline, and any notes that matter. The more context you give upfront, the easier it is to quote the job accurately.

  • Upload or send your STL/3MF file if available
  • Tell us the material, color, and deadline
  • Mention if the part has a functional purpose or tight fit requirement
  • Use the calculator first if you want a rough estimate

After You Submit

What Happens After You Submit?

After you submit a request, UCF Prints reviews the actual file instead of relying only on the calculator. This matters because two prints with the same weight can have very different support needs, print times, failure risks, and deadline constraints. Once the job is reviewed, you'll receive a confirmed quote. If you approve the quote and pay, the print is added to the queue.

Pickup

Pickup and Special Accommodations

Standard pickup is handled through scheduled campus pickup windows. The default pickup is 12 noon directly in front of the Student Union on your assigned pickup day. The goal is to avoid random meetups throughout the day and keep orders organized.

  • Default pickup time/location: 12 noon directly in front of the Student Union
  • Pickup day/window is confirmed when your order is approved
  • Payment is collected after the final quote through Zelle or PayPal
  • Rush, after-hours, delivery, or special meeting requests may be available
  • Special accommodations may add an extra fee depending on distance, urgency, or scheduling difficulty
  • If you need something unusual, mention it in your quote request

Special accommodations are not guaranteed, but we'll try to work with you when possible. Extra fees may apply.

Calculator vs quote

Why the Quote Is Not Always the Calculator Price

The calculator is meant to give a quick estimate. Final pricing may change based on supports, print orientation, failed-print risk, tight deadlines, large parts, unusual geometry, or special pickup needs.

  • Support-heavy models may cost more
  • Rush deadlines may cost more
  • Very large prints may require a custom quote
  • Special pickup or delivery requests may cost extra
  • Files with high failure risk may need review before approval
